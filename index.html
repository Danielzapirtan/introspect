About Me

Hello, I'm Dr. Emily Sanders, a licensed clinical psychologist with over 10 years of experience helping individuals navigate life's challenges and achieve mental wellness.

I received my doctorate from Stanford University and have dedicated my career to providing evidence-based therapies that empower my clients to make positive changes in their lives.

My practice is built on the principles of empathy, respect, and confidentiality. I believe that everyone has the capacity for growth and healing, and I'm committed to creating a safe space where that transformation can occur.

My Therapeutic Approach

I utilize an integrative approach, drawing from several evidence-based modalities to tailor treatment to your unique needs:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Identifying and changing unhelpful thought patterns and behaviors
  • Mindfulness-Based Therapies: Developing present-moment awareness to reduce stress and enhance wellbeing
  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT): Cultivating psychological flexibility and values-based living
  • Solution-Focused Brief Therapy: Building on your strengths and resources to create practical solutions

Regardless of the techniques used, our therapeutic relationship will always be built on trust, empathy, and collaboration. You are the expert on your own life, and my role is to provide guidance, support, and evidence-based strategies to help you achieve your goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does each therapy session last? +
Standard sessions are 50 minutes. Extended sessions (80 minutes) are available for couples counseling or intensive work when needed.
How often will we meet? +
Most clients begin with weekly sessions to establish momentum. As progress is made, we may transition to bi-weekly or monthly sessions.
Do you accept insurance? +
I am an out-of-network provider. I can provide you with documentation to submit to your insurance company for possible reimbursement.
What is your cancellation policy? +
I require 24 hours notice for cancellations. Appointments cancelled with less than 24 hours notice will be charged the full session fee.
How do I know if therapy is working? +
We'll regularly check in on your progress toward the goals we establish together. Many clients report feeling better within the first few sessions.
